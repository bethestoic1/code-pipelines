Best hosting for side projects and dev portfolios 2026

2026-03-02 · Code Pipelines

Where to host side projects, portfolios, and AI-powered apps in 2026 without overpaying or over-engineering. We break down the options by project type, stack, and budget—with specific recommendations.

What developers need from hosting

Devs need: simple deploy (git push or CI → live), fast builds and cold starts, clear pricing with no surprise bills, and custom domains with SSL. For side projects and portfolios, you don't need Kubernetes—you need a platform that stays out of the way. The right choice depends on your stack, how much you want to manage, and whether you're hosting static sites or full-stack apps with databases.

Top options for 2026

Static / JAMstack (free to start): Vercel and Netlify are the default for static sites, Next.js, and serverless functions. Git push → preview → deploy. Free tiers are generous for personal projects. Cloudflare Pages is another strong option with global CDN and zero egress fees.

Full-stack / Node / Python / AI apps: Railway and Render offer managed hosting with databases, background jobs, and easy scaling. For more control and better pricing at scale, a VPS from Vultr gives you a full server starting at $2.50–6/mo. Vultr is especially strong for AI/ML workloads—they offer GPU instances and high-performance compute alongside standard VPS. New Vultr users get $300 in credits →

WordPress / PHP: Kinsta and Cloudways are the top managed WordPress hosts for developers. Both handle caching, CDN, and scaling so you don't have to. Good for portfolios, client work, and content sites.

Picking by project type

Portfolio or docs: Vercel or Netlify (free tier). Deploy from GitHub in minutes. Add a custom domain and you're done.

Side project with a database: Railway or Render for managed simplicity. A Vultr VPS if you want full control and lower cost at scale.

AI-powered app or API: Vultr (GPU instances for inference) or Railway/Render for simpler deployments. For the full path from coding to production, see deploying AI apps to production.

Client or agency work: Managed WordPress (Kinsta, Cloudways) with staging environments and CDN included.

When to upgrade from free tiers

Free tiers work until they don't: cold starts, build limits, and bandwidth caps hit when real users show up. The move is usually free tier → $5–10/mo managed service (Railway, Render) or → a small VPS ($5–6/mo on Vultr) when you need more control. Budget $10–20/mo for any project with real traffic; you'll sleep better.
